02 / Theory

Quantum Principles

|ψ⟩

Superposition

A qubit exists in a superposition of states |0⟩ and |1⟩ simultaneously, described by the state vector |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, where |α|² + |β|² = 1. This fundamental quantum property enables genuine randomness.

|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
⟨x|ψ⟩

Wave Function Collapse

Measuring a qubit forces it to collapse irreversibly into a definite state. The probability of observing |0⟩ or |1⟩ is governed by Born's rule — P(0) = |α|², P(1) = |β|² — making outcomes intrinsically unpredictable.

P(x) = |⟨x|ψ⟩|²
H = 1/√2

Hadamard Gate

Applying the Hadamard gate to a qubit in state |0⟩ creates a perfect 50/50 superposition. This is the foundation of quantum random number generation — each measurement yields an unbiased random bit.

H|0⟩ = (|0⟩ + |1⟩)/√2
H(X)

Quantum Entropy

Unlike classical pseudo-random generators (PRNGs) that are deterministic, quantum randomness is fundamentally non-deterministic. Shannon entropy H(X) = −Σ p(x) log₂ p(x) is maximized at 1 bit per qubit measurement.

H(X) = −Σ pᵢ log₂ pᵢ
